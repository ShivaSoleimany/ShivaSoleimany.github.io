Introduction
The current pilot training programs require pilots who are in high demand in Canada. The ultimate goal of this research is to decrease the needed help from pilots during the training procedure of new pilots. This objective, once reached, will have a positive impact on the entire Canadian aviation industry. Research along this direction will also help in significantly lowering the cost of pilot training program by reducing the number of additional flight instructors required to produce the required numbers of new pilots. Additionally, this technology can potentially be used in a number of related tutoring tasks. The first phase of this project will focus on using ML to identify mistakes or sub-optimal maneuvers of trainee pilots inside a flight simulator (Xplane), with the goal of reducing human trainer supervision while improving student learning outcomes. To learn how to fly, trainees at DTC are currently guided through exercises in a flight simulator by a human flying instructor. We posit that training a predictive/RL-enabled system to take on some tasks is a viable approach to reduce the workload of an instructor and allow them to interact with more students simultaneously. In the first step, an RL agent has been trained with SAC algorithm to perform the most important task in flying an airplane, straight and level.
